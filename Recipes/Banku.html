Banku and Okro Stew

Banku with Okro Stew

Description

One of Ghana’s amazing dish enjoyed by many household. This meal is one of the best,
it is delicious and tasty. Banku can be eaten with different kinds of soup like Palm nut soup, Groundnut soup, Goat light soup

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of Corn Dough
  • 1/2 cup of Cassava Dough
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup Okra, diced
  • 1 pound meat or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions
  • 4 medium-sized tomatoes
  • 4 pieces Kpakpo shito pepper
  • 1 inch root Ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1/4 tablespoons herrings
  • Cowhide (Wele)
  • 1/2 cup Palm Oil or Vegetable oil
  • Crabs (optional)
  • 1 salted fish(momoni)
  • Seasoning cube or powder

Steps in Cooking Banku and Okro Stew

  1. In a large bowl, mix Cassava dough with moderate water and strain out the lumps using colander into pot or saucepan
  2. After straining into pot, add corn dough, add extra water and mix together using your hands
    until smooth and slightly thick mixture is formed. Add salt to taste
  3. Under medium heat, place mixture on fire. Stir consistently until the mixture starts to boil.
  4. Once it begins boiling, use a spatula(Banku ‘ta’) to knead the mixture to prevent it from forming lumps.
  5. Continuously knead the mixture intermittently until it gets smoother
    and thicker and its colour changes slightly for about 15 minutes.
  6. Banku is ready when you don’t taste the raw corn dough after eating
    or you can mash a little of the cooked mixture in a little water. If it dissolves and turns milky then your
    Banku isn’t ready.
  7. When the Banku is ready, scoop it out and wrap each ball in a plain polythene rubber.
  8. Banku can be kept for about two-three days in an airtight container
    or longer period in a fridge
  9. Wash chopped meat, crabs and cowhide in a large bowl.
  10. Wash and cut into pieces 1 onion, garlic, pepper and ginger. Blend until
    smooth. Reserve some for stew
  11. lace meat, crabs and wele in a saucepan, add a little water, salt, spices
    and bring to a boil. Pour in blended ingredients(reserve 1/4 cup) and steam for about 10 mins
    until tender.
  12. In a separate pot, place chopped okro, add a cup of water and let it simmer
    for about 8 mins. Remove from fire and allow it to cool.
  13. Now pour some palm oil into another saucepan. Add salted fish and sliced onions.
    Cook for about 3 mins
  14. Add reserved blended kpakpo shito(pepper), ginger and garlic and allow to simmer
    for about 5 mins.
  15. Wash and chop tomatoes and add to the sauce.
  16. Wash your herrings and take out the bones, add to the sauce and let it simmer for
    about 5 mins.
  17. Now add your steamed beef, and cowhide (wele), stir and cook for a while
  18. Add your cooked okro and stir to mix evenly, reduce the heat and allow it to cook for
    about 10 mins. Add some salt to your preferred taste.
